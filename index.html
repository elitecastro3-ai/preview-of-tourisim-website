About Masaka

The Green City of Uganda

Masaka City lies in the heart of central Buganda — the vibrant land of the Kabaka. Often called "The Green City," Masaka offers rolling hills, lush swamps, and sparkling water bodies. Its proximity to Lake Victoria, Africa's largest lake, provides breathtaking shores and world-class water activities.

Explore the tranquil forests surrounding the city, including the renowned Kwewayo Forest and Nabajjuzi Wetland, ideal for birdwatching, nature walks, and unforgettable road trips through Uganda's most scenic landscapes.

Explore Masaka

Top Attractions & Landmarks

From ancient royal palaces to serene lakeshores, Masaka City offers experiences that captivate every kind of traveller.

History

Masaka Fort – First Colonial Office

Step back in time at the Masaka Fort, a colonial-era relic that witnessed the struggles of World War II. The city bears vivid memories of the 1979 Liberation War — the battle that overthrew Idi Amin. Visit memorial sites where the Tanzania-Uganda war reshaped the nation's destiny.

📍 Masaka Town
⏰ Dawn to Dusk
Culture

Kabaka's Palace, Nkoni

Masaka City lies in the heart of central Buganda, the land of the Kabaka. It is home to the Kabaka's Palace at Nkoni, the historic Sazza grounds, the royal Butikkilo, and a museum filled with Buganda's treasured artefacts. Come and experience the living culture of the Buganda Kingdom.

📍 Nkoni, Masaka
👑 Cultural Site
Nature

Nabugabo Sand Beach

Discover the pristine shores of Lake Nabugabo, one of Uganda's most peaceful lakeside retreats. With sparkling water, white sandy beaches and a tranquil atmosphere, it offers breathtaking swimming, boating, and relaxation away from the city hustle.

📍 Kalisizo Area
🏖️ Beach & Water
Nature

Nabajjuzi Ramzer Site

Explore the Nabajjuzi Wetland, a Ramsar-listed conservation site of global ecological importance. Perfect for birdwatching with hundreds of rare species, nature walks through lush vegetation, and discovering the biodiversity that makes Masaka City a true green destination.

📍 Masaka Outskirts
🦅 Birdwatching
Religion

Kitovu Cathedral

Masaka City is a renowned spiritual destination. At Kitovu Cathedral, the faithful gather for mass and reflection. Beside it lies the grave of Msgr. Joseph Ngobya, a beloved priest where pilgrims pray and receive blessings. Nearby, the tomb of Sister Amadeo draws countless visitors seeking intercession, good luck, and answered prayers.

📍 Kitovu, Masaka
🕊️ Spiritual Site
Agriculture

Coffee & Banana Plantations

As a commercial hub, Masaka thrives on agriculture. The region is famous for its robust coffee plantations, producing some of Uganda's finest Arabica and Robusta beans. Endless expanses of banana (matooke) plantations form the backbone of local cuisine and economy, creating a scenic green carpet across the hills.

📍 Masaka Region
🌱 Eco-Tourism
Annual Events

Celebrate with Masaka City

Two of Uganda's most thrilling annual events call Masaka home — drawing visitors from across the nation and beyond.

⚽ Annual Football Tournament

Buganda Masaza Cup

Each year, the Buganda Kingdom's 18 counties battle for glory in the Masaza Cup — the region's most thrilling football tournament. Buddu County, the heart of Masaka City, competes with fierce pride and has lifted the trophy three times. The matches bring massive crowds from the entire region, boosting local tourism, hospitality, and cultural pride.

3× Champions – Buddu County
🏃 International Sports Event

Masaka Marathon

Every year, Masaka City hosts the Masaka Marathon — an international sporting event attracting runners from across Uganda and around the world. This annual race promotes tourism and showcases the city's scenic hills and welcoming spirit. The marathon is jointly organised by Masaka City Council and the Buganda Kingdom, with Prince David Wasajja serving as chief runner. This year's theme: "Running for Disabled" — a fundraising effort to support children with disabilities.

🎗️ Theme: Running for Disabled

Local Flavours

Must-Try Foods in Masaka

Masaka's food scene is deeply rooted in Buganda culture — rich, hearty, and unforgettable. Here's what to eat when you visit.

🍌
Matooke
The iconic Ugandan staple — steamed green bananas served with groundnut sauce, meat, or vegetables. A true taste of home.
🐟
Grilled Tilapia
Fresh from Lake Victoria, grilled whole with herbs and served with ugali or fries. A lakeside delicacy not to be missed.
🌯
Rolex
Uganda's beloved street food — a chapati rolled with eggs, vegetables, and tomatoes. Fresh, fast, and utterly delicious.
☕
Masaka Coffee
Sip the finest Arabica and Robusta beans grown on Masaka's lush hills. Rich, bold, and aromatic — Uganda's pride in a cup.
🥜
Groundnut Stew
A creamy, rich stew made from ground peanuts, slow-cooked with vegetables and served over rice or matooke.
🍢
Muchomo (Roasted Meat)
Skewered and roasted over open charcoal, muchomo is the go-to street food at every Masaka gathering and event.

Your Travel Guide to Masaka

Everything you need to plan a seamless, unforgettable trip to Masaka City.

🚌
Getting There
  • 138 km southwest of Kampala
  • ~2 hr drive via Kampala-Masaka Highway
  • Regular coach buses from Kampala's taxi parks
  • Shared taxis (matatus) available daily
  • Private hire cars recommended for comfort
📅
Best Time to Visit
  • Dry seasons: Jan–Feb & Jun–Aug ideal
  • Masaka Marathon — annual (check dates)
  • Buganda Masaza Cup — annual football
  • Avoid heavy rains: March–May & Oct–Nov
  • Cultural festivals year-round
💰
Budget Estimates
  • Bus from Kampala: ~UGX 15,000–25,000
  • Budget guesthouse: from UGX 50,000/night
  • Mid-range hotel: UGX 150,000–300,000/night
  • Local meal: UGX 5,000–15,000
  • Boda boda (moto): UGX 2,000–8,000 per trip
🚲
Getting Around
  • Boda bodas (motorcycle taxis) everywhere
  • Special hire taxis for day trips
  • Cycling tours around the hills
  • Walking — city centre is compact
  • Car hire available from Kampala
🛡️
Safety Tips
  • Masaka is generally safe for tourists
  • Keep valuables secure in busy markets
  • Use registered boda bodas
  • Travel in daylight for remote areas
  • Carry a local SIM (MTN/Airtel) for maps
🤝
Local Etiquette
  • Greet with respect — say "Oli Otya" (hello)
  • Dress modestly when visiting religious sites
  • Ask permission before photographing people
  • Remove shoes when entering homes
  • Embrace the slow, warm Ugandan hospitality
